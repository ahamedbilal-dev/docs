This entire course is free, and the only thing that keeps it visible to new learners is engagement from people like you. If the course helped you — even a little — there's one simple thing you can do that makes a real difference: leave a like and a comment on YouTube.

## Why your feedback matters

YouTube's algorithm decides who sees this course based on engagement signals. Every like tells the algorithm the video is worth recommending. Every comment telling other learners what you built or what clicked for you helps them decide whether to start. It costs you 30 seconds, but it could be the reason someone else starts their Python journey.

## Share with others

Know someone who wants to learn Python but doesn't know where to start? Send them the course. Share it in communities, Discord servers, or LinkedIn. Helping one person discover a free resource takes 30 seconds and could change their career.

## Leave feedback on GitHub

Found a bug, a broken link, or something that could be explained more clearly? Open an issue or submit a pull request on the course GitHub repository. The course is open-source and improvements from the community make it better for everyone.

<Tip>
  Contributing to an open-source project — even just fixing a typo — is a great
  first GitHub contribution. It's also something you can mention when talking to
  employers.
</Tip>

## That's all I ask

Liking the video and leaving a comment is my only ask if you found value in this course. It keeps the content free and helps more people find it. Thank you for learning, for building, and for being part of this community.
